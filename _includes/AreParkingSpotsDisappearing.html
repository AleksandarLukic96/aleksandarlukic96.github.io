Are Parking Spots Disappearing?

Is something rotten in the state of Copenhagen, when it comes to car parking spots? Well, it is not such an easy question to answer. The number of parking spots in the city has been fluctuating over the years and as some spots disappear, others are created.

If you were interested, these are the [NUMBER] current registered parking spots in Copenhagen.

Of course, we do not suggest using this figure to try and find a parking spot in Copenhagen. There are much more appropriate tools for that, such as the app EasyPark or the government website kbhkort.kk.dk.

So, since this map isn't really helping, let's try something else.

Perhaps the reason parking seems to be so difficult to find is that the parking spaces are actually being moved around or changed. Every year, parking spaces are being changed and drivers might find it difficult to figure out where parking can now be found or if they can even use said parking space. [SOURCES]. What can be seen on the next figure, is the amount of parking spaces that have been changed in a given year in Copenhagen. No wonder people are confused.

There are multiple reasons for this, but one of the more important ones is the rise of electric cars. People are starting to buy more and more electric cars, which is also because of Danish government incentivising this. Not just by making it less expensive to invest in electric cars, but also by increasingly reserving parking spots for electric cars and converting parking spots into charging spots. To be more precise, the number of electric cars have been increasing as shown in the following figure.

Notice how electric cars are the only cars that have been steadily increasing in the last four years.

Another reason is the centralisation and privatisation of parking spaces. Over the last 20 years, multiple parking buildings have been constructed in Copenhagen. [SOURCES]. While this has increased the overall number of parking spaces, a lot of parking spaces on street level have been removed [SOURCE], which leads to the feeling that there is no apparent parking.
